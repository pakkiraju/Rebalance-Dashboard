Flow Measure Definitions

Net Flow

Buys minus Sells. Shows the directional pressure. Positive = net buying, Negative = net selling.

Simple Gross Flow

Buys plus Sells. Shows total two-way activity regardless of direction.

Gross Ex-Crossing Flow

Maximum of Buys and Sells. Represents the flow that cannot be crossed internally and must hit the market. This is the most relevant measure for estimating market impact.

Example: If AAPL has $500M buys and $300M sells, then Net = +$200M, Simple Gross = $800M, Gross Ex-Crossing = $500M.
